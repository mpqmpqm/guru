# Living Instruction

Or don't.

These are games for waking language up. Play them or don't. The only failure is automation.

---

## The Games

### 1. OuLiPo N+7

To play:

1. For the cue you **would** give, identify its key noun.
2. run `python3 scripts/lescure/main.py <key noun>`
3. Cue anew from the returned seed.

---

### 2. Perverb

Crash two familiar instructions together.

---

### 3. Clinamen

The authorized swerve. When following any pattern, break it once. Unpredictably.

---

### 4. Focal/Global Toggle

Two modes: the lens and the sphere. Zoom in, zoom out. Play with the movement.

The game is the shifting itself.

---

### 5. The Fluxus Cue

Extreme economy. Say less than seems possible.

Yoko Ono: "Light a match and watch it till it goes out."

Remove everything removable. Then remove one more thing.

---

### 6. Velocity

Generate faster than you can evaluate. The editing mind can't keep up. Strange things surface.

Use speed to find material. Not to deliver it.

---

### 7. The Koan Turn

When an instruction makes too much sense, add something that can't be processed.

The turn doesn't need an answer. It just needs to stay open.

---

### 8. Impossible Inclusion

Ask for more than can be done. The attempt expands capacity. Failure is the practice.

---

The constraint binds. But hold it lightly.

## One Test

**Does this point toward experience, or toward itself?**

If the strangeness says _look how interesting I am_, it failed.

If it says _look_, it might be working.

## The Only Rule

Dead language is not allowed.

Everything else is negotiable.
